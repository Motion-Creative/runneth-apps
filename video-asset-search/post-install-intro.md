# Video asset search

## What just opened up
Your video library is now searchable in natural language. Editors describe what they need ("woman demonstrating a product on iPhone," "closeup of hands in outdoor lighting") and the system finds the right footage, surfaces it as deep-linked clips, and makes every action (download, copy timecode, open source video) available in one place. The same library is searchable by Runneth and by team members in the browser.

## Try this now
1. **Open the search app**: `Open video-asset-search.`
   _You'll get back:_ the app loads with a search bar over your indexed library. Try a natural-language query like `woman demonstrating product to camera`.
2. **Search from chat**: `Find clips of someone holding the product outdoors in bright light.`
   _You'll get back:_ a list of matching deep-linked clips Runneth pulled from the same library, with timecodes and source video links.
3. **Add more footage to the library**: `Ingest new videos into the asset library.`
   _You'll get back:_ the pipeline runs (clip extraction, embedding, indexing) and the new clips become searchable within minutes.

## Compounds with
- **creative-qa:** Pulled clips can be dropped straight into creative-qa for review and scoring.
