# Review library

## What just opened up
Customer reviews now live in the brain as structured, scored markdown files Runneth can read. Every review gets tagged for creative-strategy value, organized by product, and indexed for retrieval. When you ask about pain points, language patterns, or feature emphasis, the answers come from real customer voice instead of inference.

## Try this now
1. **Pull reviews from a connected source**: `Pull the latest reviews into the library.`
   _You'll get back:_ reviews fetched from the configured source, scored, tagged, and written to `/agent/brain/reviews/` with a fresh `_index.md`.
2. **Mine the library for an angle**: `What language do customers use when describing the moment they decide to buy?`
   _You'll get back:_ direct quotes, score-weighted, grouped by pattern, sourced from specific review files.
3. **Find the strongest objection signal**: `What's the most common objection in our reviews and how do customers describe it in their own words?`
   _You'll get back:_ the dominant objection, frequency, and verbatim phrases you can borrow for hook copy or landing-page CRO.

## Compounds with
- **brand-audit:** Review patterns feed the audience and messaging layers of the audit.
- **landing-page-summary:** Customer language from reviews sharpens the landing-page diagnostic.
