# Self-iteration loop

## What just opened up
Every recurring process you build with Runneth from now on gets a feedback loop attached. When Runneth ships a routine, an app, or any workflow that makes a recommendation, it surfaces a short offer to wire in the capture mechanism that tracks whether those recommendations were right or wrong. Over time those processes get measurably better instead of fading into background noise.

## Try this now
1. **Build something the loop can hook into**: `Build me a routine that flags fatigued ads every Friday.`
   _You'll get back:_ the routine, followed automatically by a one-sentence offer to add a feedback capture so the fatigue calls get tracked against what actually happened.
2. **See the loop's reasoning on an existing process**: `Walk me through where a feedback loop would fit on my weekly performance deck.`
   _You'll get back:_ the assertion the deck makes, the natural intercept moment to ask about it, the right storage layer for the answer, and how the loop would compound.
3. **Decline gracefully**: `Skip the loop on this one.`
   _You'll get back:_ noted in the conversation, not re-pitched, captured in the one-pager so future turns respect the call.

## Compounds with
- **plan-mode:** Plans produce builds; the loop turns those builds into systems that compound.
