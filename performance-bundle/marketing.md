## Super powers this unlocks

- One install configures the complete analysis workflow: brand context feeds the deep dive, strategy briefs frame every performance answer, and the weekly deck composes both.
- Skills install in the correct dependency order automatically — brand-audit before creative-deep-dive, paid-strategy-audit before weekly-performance-deck.
- Every underlying skill is independently callable after install. Run a standalone competitor-analysis, fire a hook-evaluator, or refresh one channel's strategy brief without re-running the full bundle.
- Three built-in routines run automatically: Monday brand refresh, Friday strategy drift check, and Friday 2pm weekly deck.
- Every analysis skill compounds on the others. The deep dive reads the brand audit, the deck reads the strategy brief, next week's deck reads this week's snapshot.

## How it works

Installing the bundle runs each use case setup flow in sequence: brand-audit first, then paid-strategy-audit, then creative-deep-dive, then weekly-performance-deck. Each collects what it needs — brand URL, review sources, Slack channels, ad platform connections. After setup, all routines are live and the framework is ready.

Every future analysis question reads the right upstream context automatically. "Deep dive on FUN29228" reads strategy.md. "What's working in Meta prospecting?" reads the paid strategy brief. "Why is this hook weak?" runs the 10-point scoring skill. No re-running foundation work on every question.

## A real example

Jordan installs the bundle on a DTC client. Brand-audit mines the brand site, reviews, and competitors in about 2 minutes and produces the persona matrix. Paid-strategy-audit detects Meta and TikTok, runs the detective parser on the account's naming conventions, and produces a funnel-map Jordan reacts to. Creative-deep-dive installs its nine diagnostic skills. Weekly-performance-deck creates the deck app.

By Friday, the first deck is live. Jordan shares it with the client — it opens in their browser, branded in the client's colors, with deep dives on the top creatives and specific iteration recommendations for next week. Week two, the deck opens with "Last week we recommended testing whether panic-window framing or the 'like usual' normalization was carrying the hook. Here's the early read."
