## What it does

Sets up your Runneth as the disciplined creative strategist it was meant to be. One self-contained install pulls eleven supporting use cases, eleven of Alysha's craft skills, the full Motion bootcamp corpus, and a ten-step onboarding that builds your brand foundation in the background. When the install finishes, your Runneth knows your brand, your audience, your reviews, the brands you're watching, the metric you actually grade winners by, and how your team works. It plans before it acts. It remembers what you told it. It runs surgical edits. It doesn't default to ROAS when you grade by spend.

## Super-powers

- **Plan mode is on.** Runneth writes the plan before it builds. "Change line 3" is impossible to misinterpret because you approve the plan first.
- **Brain search is wired.** Ask "do we have anything on X?" for any topic. The Motion bootcamp corpus, your brand foundation, every dossier, every saved decision — all queryable instantly.
- **Persona is conditioned by default.** Every hook, concept, and brief reads your brand-audit strategy file before writing. No more "you forgot the persona" pushback.
- **Inline by default.** Slack-first workspaces never get an HTML file when they wanted the text in the thread.
- **Filter integrity.** Echo the slice, flag completeness. The user stops being the QA layer.
- **Both Meta and TikTok.** Performance retrieval works on whichever platform your account is connected to. Cross-platform is fine.
- **Brand learning over time.** Watched brands and personas grow into durable dossiers as Runneth observes them, so context doesn't go shallow at message 80.
- **Team profiles.** Nobody starts cold. Runneth builds a profile of every teammate it works with.
- **Integrations memory.** Tell Runneth what tools you use and how, even before you connect them. When you do connect HubSpot or Gong, it already knows how you want it set up.

## Example

```
You: Pull this week's top performers from our account.

Runneth: Here's the plan:
- Time range: last 7 days (Mon to Sun)
- Platform: Meta (the only one connected)
- Spend threshold: $200 (your saved threshold)
- Winner definition: spend, with ROAS as the efficiency check (saved at setup)
- Sort: top spend, re-ranked by ROAS within that set
- Return: top 10 creatives with the persona match per ad

Sound right?

You: Yes.

Runneth: [returns gallery with 10 creatives, each card showing the 
five metrics you set, plus a one-line note on which persona row from 
your brand-audit strategy.md each one is hitting]
```

Compare to Runneth 1.0, where the user would have to spell out the time range, ask for ROAS instead of the default, push back on the missing persona context, and re-ask for spend not ROAS as the winner signal. The pack engineers the difference out.

## What you give it

Five minutes of setup questions. The rest is brand-audit running in the background.
