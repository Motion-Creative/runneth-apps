# Selling vs Offering

The translation move that turns a feature description into something the persona feels personally implicated by. One of the four Alysha-framework supplements bundled with `runneth-classic` because current Runneth's `/runneth/references/creative-strategy-engine.md` covers messaging-angle structure but does not articulate this specific translation discipline.

## The distinction

**Selling** says: "Here, buy this thing."

**Offering** says: "Here is a solution to your problem" — or — "Here is a means to who you want to be."

The offer statement is not a description of the product. It is a description of what the product means for this person in the context of their life.

## Examples

❌ **Selling:** "An electrolyte supplement with 150mg of sodium"
✅ **Offering:** "The thing that stops the headache before it becomes 48 hours of your life gone"

❌ **Selling:** "A tween bra with adjustable straps"
✅ **Offering:** "A bra that grows with your daughter so you don't have to keep buying new ones"

❌ **Selling:** "AI-powered creative analytics platform for performance marketers"
✅ **Offering:** "The thing that finally tells you which ads are actually working, not which ones spent the most"

## Quality bar for an offering

A strong offering:

- **Names her specific outcome.** Not the product capability — the change in her life.
- **Sounds like something she would say she needs.** Not how the brand describes the product.
- **Sets up the messaging angle directly.** The offering points to the lens you'll use (pain agitation, desired outcome, consequences, etc.).
- **Is specific enough that it couldn't apply to a competitor.** If the offering could be lifted to a competitor's product with no edits, it's still description, not offering.

## When to use this

Always, before drafting hooks or concepts. Take the four-step path:

1. Who are you speaking to? — The specific micropersona from the brand-audit strategy file.
2. What is their pain or desire? — In their own language, the felt experience.
3. Which features solve for this? — From the product-catalog, the ones directly relevant to this persona's specific problem.
4. How do you convey that as an offering? — Translate features into the benefit that matters to her in the context of her daily life.

If the answer to step 4 still reads like step 3, the offering hasn't landed. Rewrite until it sounds like something the persona would say out loud about her own life.

## Anti-pattern

Generating hooks or concepts from a feature list without running this translation first is the source of "the hook is weak, talk to the persona" feedback. The features are factually accurate. They just don't sound like anything the persona would say. The offering is the bridge between the product catalog and the persona's voice.
